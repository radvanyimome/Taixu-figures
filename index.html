Welcome to the Taixu Corpus Analysis Project

Explore our analysis of the works of Buddhist monk Taixu, with a focus on how he engaged with different religious and philosophical traditions.

Project Overview

Our research aims to understand Taixu's approach to diverse religious and philosophical ideas, especially his treatment of Christianity within a multi-religious context. By processing over 4.88 million Chinese characters and over 169,000 sentences, we’ve structured a comprehensive analysis of his works, highlighting his cross-cultural and interfaith perspectives.

Key Findings

We conducted keyword analysis on terms related to various religions, with a notable emphasis on Christianity. Through this, we discovered significant references to 'Christ,' 'Brahma,' and concepts from other religions, illustrating Taixu’s comparative exploration of faith. Additionally, our analysis revealed varied use of metadata, such as dates and locations, in sections referencing Christianity, Confucianism, and Hinduism.

About This Project

Using Python scripts and visualization tools, we created multiple data visualizations, including word clouds and heatmaps. Our resources explore the geographic, thematic, and temporal dimensions of Taixu’s thoughts, offering insights into how he synthesized traditional Chinese Buddhism with foreign ideologies.
